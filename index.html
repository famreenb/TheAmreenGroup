Welcome To The AMREEN Group

Brief Introduction

The AMREEN (Advanced Materials Research for Energy Engineering Nanomaterials) group, at the Research Institute for Sustainable Energy (RISE), TCG CREST, is dedicated to exploring and designing advanced materials for next-generation energy storage solutions. Our research focuses on understanding and developing innovative battery components using cutting-edge computational techniques. We specialize in the design and optimization of layered oxide cathodes, anodes, and electrolytes—both liquid and solid—to enhance the efficiency, safety, and sustainability of energy storage systems. To achieve this, we leverage powerful computational methods such as Density Functional Theory (DFT), Classical and Ab Initio Molecular Dynamics (AIMD), and Machine Learning. These approaches allow us to study materials at the atomic level, predict their behavior, and identify promising candidates for high-performance batteries. By combining fundamental physics with AI-driven methodologies, we aim to accelerate the discovery of new materials and improve existing technologies, making energy storage more reliable and environmentally friendly. In this group, we are committed to pushing the boundaries of materials research to support cleaner energy solutions. Our work contributes to global efforts in developing sustainable and high-efficiency batteries that can power the future of renewable energy, electric vehicles, and grid storage. Through collaboration, innovation, and rigorous scientific exploration, we strive to create impactful advancements that will drive progress in energy engineering and sustainability.

Research Areas

Electrolyte Design

We study non-aqueous electrolytes and interfacial behavior to understand ion transport mechanisms and electrochemical stability across different electrodes. In this approach we use molecular dynamics (Classical as well as AIMD). Machine learning assisted MD simulations were also considered for effective analysis of CEI/SEI interfaces.

Machine Learning Approach

We use available ML interatomic potentials (MLIP) and perform benchmarking analysis to see which MLIPs are more efficient in our studies to accelerate materials discovery and property prediction for batteries, reducing computational costs.

High-Entropy Materials

We explore compositional complexity using a combination of DFT, ML, and MD simulations to identify high-entropy electrode materials with improved stability and performance.
